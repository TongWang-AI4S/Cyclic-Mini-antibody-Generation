# Cyclic-Mini-antibody-Generation

The workflow of generating good cyclic mini-antibody from wild-type protein segments 

Recurrent missense mutations in the Human Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor 2 (HER2) extracellular domain, specifically the S310F variant, drive oncogenesis and confer clinical resistance to Pertuzumab by sterically hindering its binding interface. To address this and the inherent limitations of monoclonal antibodies, such as poor tumor penetration, we developed an in silico strategy to engineer cyclic "mini-antibody" peptides derived from Pertuzumab’s CDR2 loops. By employing a population-based Bayesian Active Learning pipeline, we iteratively optimized these scaffolds for binding affinity and aqueous stability. By adopting heuristic mutation guided by protein language model and training a Bayesian surrogate model on AlphaFold3 embedding, we expect that we yield engineered cyclic peptides capable of disrupting the HER2 dimerization interface and overcoming mutation-driven therapeutic resistance.
